What I do

I lead the PEO advisory practice at IMA Financial Group, an employee-owned, top-tier independent brokerage. My team helps companies evaluate whether a Professional Employer Organization is the right move, which PEO actually fits, and what to negotiate before signing.

This site, PEO Clarify, is the independent voice in the PEO comparison space. Most PEO comparison content online is run by PEOs or by affiliate marketers who get paid when you sign. We don't operate that way. We compare every option, we get paid by the carriers and providers you eventually select, and we always tell you when a PEO is not the right answer.

Why PEOs matter, and why most companies pick the wrong one

For a company with 5 to 150 employees, a PEO can be transformative. Better health benefits than you could ever buy on the open market. Compliance infrastructure that scales. Access to 401(k), workers comp, EPLI, and HR support without hiring a department. Done right, a PEO buys you back time, retention, and benefits leverage you would never have alone.

Done wrong, a PEO becomes a $200,000 to $500,000 annual line item with no transparency. The pricing is opaque. The contracts have auto-renewal clauses with 60 to 90 day notice windows. Co-employment exposure is rarely explained. By the time the CFO is paying attention, the company is locked into another year.

The decision matters. The PEO you pick matters even more. That's why this practice exists.

How I work with clients

  • Free assessment. A 20-minute call. I'll tell you whether a PEO actually fits your business, what it should cost, and which providers fit your industry, geography, and growth stage.
  • Independent comparison. If a PEO is the right move, my team requests proposals from our curated panel of 6 partners. We normalize the quotes (PEOs deliberately obscure direct comparison) and present a real apples-to-apples view.
  • Contract review and negotiation. Every PEO contract has negotiable clauses. We tell you which ones. We negotiate on your behalf at no additional cost to you.
  • Ongoing service. If you sign with a PEO through us, we stay in the loop. Renewals, service issues, claims advocacy, exit planning if it ever comes to that.
  • Compensation. I am paid standard broker commissions by the PEO you select, never by you. Going through an advisor does not increase your cost. Often it lowers it.
